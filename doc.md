# 🇺🇸 US Healthcare – Quick Reference (for India Audience)

---

## 1️⃣ System Overview (Mental Model)

**What defines US healthcare?**

* Insurance-driven system
* Strong focus on **cost control + outcomes**
* Highly **data-driven and integrated**
* Multiple intermediaries (unlike India)

👉 Key idea:

> Healthcare is not just treatment — it is a **financial ecosystem managing risk and cost**

---

## 2️⃣ Core Terminology – The “3 Ps” (+1)

### **Payer**

* Insurance companies that **pay for healthcare**
* Examples:

  * UnitedHealth Group
  * Humana

👉 Business interest:

* Reduce total cost of care
* Improve quality scores → earn government bonuses

---

### **Provider**

* Doctors, hospitals, clinics delivering care
* Example:

  * Mayo Clinic

👉 Business interest:

* Get paid for services
* Earn incentives under value-based care

---

### **Patient**

* End consumer of healthcare services

👉 Business reality:

* Pays premiums + co-pay
* Data contributes to system-wide analytics

---

### **PBM (Pharmacy Benefit Manager) – The Hidden 4th Player**

* Controls **drug pricing, access, and pharmacy network**

Examples:

* OptumRx
* CVS Caremark
* Express Scripts

👉 Business interest:

* Earn from rebates, contracts, and data
* Control which drugs are used (formulary power)

---

## 3️⃣ Key Government Entity

### **CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services)**

* US government body managing public insurance programs

👉 Role:

* Pays insurers
* Defines quality metrics
* Runs **Star Ratings system**

👉 Business impact:

* Incentivizes entire ecosystem through **performance-linked payments**

---

## 4️⃣ Value-Based Care (VBC)

### Definition

> Payment model where healthcare providers and insurers are rewarded based on **patient outcomes**, not volume of services.

---

### Traditional vs VBC

| Model            | Payment Basis     |
| ---------------- | ----------------- |
| Fee-for-service  | Per visit/test    |
| Value-based care | Based on outcomes |

---

### In Practice

* If patients stay healthy → system earns more
* If patients worsen → financial penalties

---

### Business Impact

| Stakeholder | Incentive                          |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Payers      | Lower claims cost + bonus from CMS |
| Providers   | Incentives for good outcomes       |
| PBMs        | Improve adherence → better metrics |
| Vendors     | Paid for improving performance     |

---

## 5️⃣ CMS Star Ratings

* Rating system: ⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
* Applies to insurers (Medicare plans)

### Key Metrics:

* Medication adherence
* Chronic disease control
* Patient satisfaction

👉 Impact:

* Higher rating → **higher government payments + more enrollments**

---

## 6️⃣ Medication Management (MTM)

### Definition

> Structured review of a patient’s medications to ensure safety, effectiveness, and adherence.

---

### Full Form:

* **MTM = Medication Therapy Management**

---

### What It Includes

* Medication review (all prescriptions)
* Identifying:

  * Drug interactions
  * Side effects
  * Duplicate therapy
* Checking adherence
* Cost optimization (generic alternatives)

---

### Who Performs It

* Pharmacists (often via call centers)
* MTM vendors

Examples:

* Tabula Rasa HealthCare
* OutcomesMTM

---

### Business Interest

| Player      | Benefit                  |
| ----------- | ------------------------ |
| Payers      | Reduce hospital costs    |
| CMS         | Better population health |
| Pharmacists | Additional revenue       |
| Providers   | Incent payments          |
| PBMs        | Better adherence metrics |

---

## 7️⃣ Medication Adherence

### Definition

> Whether patients take medicines as prescribed

---

### Key Metric:

* **PDC (Proportion of Days Covered)**

  * Target: ≥ 80%

---

### How It Is Measured

* Pharmacy refill data (via PBMs)
* Claims data (via insurers)

---

### Why It Matters

* Directly impacts:

  * CMS Star Ratings
  * Insurer revenue
  * Patient outcomes

---

## 8️⃣ MTM & Adherence Operations

### Two Types of Activities

#### 1. Adherence Outreach

* Reminder calls / SMS
* Often automated or agent-driven

#### 2. Medication Review (CMR)

* Detailed pharmacist call
* Structured questionnaire

---

### Data Collected

* Missed doses
* Cost issues
* Side effects
* Behavioral insights

👉 Insight:

> These programs are also **data collection pipelines**

---

## 9️⃣ Money Flow (Simplified)

1. CMS → pays insurers (per patient)
2. Insurers → pay:

   * PBMs (drug management)
   * MTM vendors (calls/reviews)
   * Providers (review incentives)
3. PBMs:

   * Pay pharmacies
   * Receive rebates from pharma companies

---

### Key Insight

> Money is spent upfront to **avoid larger future medical costs**

---

## 🔟 Role of PBMs (Deep Insight Section)

PBMs like OptumRx:

* Control drug pricing
* Decide drug availability
* Track adherence
* Manage pharmacy network
* Operate mail-order pharmacies

👉 Strategic position:

> They sit at the **center of money + data + drug flow**

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ Data as a Core Asset

### Who Holds What

| Entity      | Data Type                  |
| ----------- | -------------------------- |
| PBMs        | Prescription + refill data |
| Insurers    | Claims + patient risk      |
| Providers   | Clinical data              |
| MTM vendors | Behavioral insights        |

---

### Insight

> Data drives:

* Risk scoring
* Premium pricing
* Care decisions

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ India vs USA (Contextual Understanding)

| Aspect             | India         | USA              |
| ------------------ | ------------- | ---------------- |
| Payment            | Out-of-pocket | Insurance-driven |
| Drug pricing       | Market-based  | PBM-controlled   |
| Adherence tracking | Minimal       | Systematic       |
| Data integration   | Low           | High             |
| Incentives         | Volume-based  | Outcome-based    |

---

## 1️⃣3️⃣ Emerging Trends

* Shift to **AI-driven adherence tracking**
* Digital engagement (apps, SMS, WhatsApp)
* Pharmacists focusing on complex cases
* Increased scrutiny on PBMs (pricing transparency)

---

## 1️⃣4️⃣ Key Takeaways

* US healthcare = **Finance + Data + Care combined**
* Medication adherence is a **financial metric**, not just clinical
* PBMs are **central power players**
* Value-Based Care aligns all players toward **cost reduction via outcomes**
